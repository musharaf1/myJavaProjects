MODIFICATIONS OF DALTON'S ATOMIC THEORY

The atomic theories proposed by Dalton did hold true for quite a long time with simple reaction, but with advancements and more experiment conducted some exceptions were discovered and thus those theories became redefined or modified.
These modifications are as follows:
1. Elements are composed of extremely small indivisible particles called atoms. Modification: the discovery of the three sub-particle of an atom which I summarize as ‘PEN’ i.e. Proton, Electron and Neutron
The history of the discovery of each of this sub-particles will be discussed later int the chapter. 2. Atoms are neither created nor destroyed during a chemical reaction but they undergo separation, combination, or rearrangement.
Modification: this is true to a large extent with simple reactions, but it is not so with nuclear reaction where an heavy radioactive nucleaus(atom) would split to form a lighter nuclei(another atom) or two lighter nuclei combine to form an heavier nuclei
The splitting of the nuclear atom is term Nuclear Fission.
The combination of the lighter nuclei to form heavier ones is termed Nuclear Fusion. Uranium for example is a radioactive element which decays as follows;
23892U 23490Th + 42He
In the above equation, the an atom(uranium) breaks down to produce another atom(Thorium) and an alpha particle(an Helium nucleus). here, an atom is destroyed and another is created.
3. All atoms of a given element are identical, having similar properties (size, mass, and chemical properties) and they differ from atoms of all other elements.
Modification: this is not so with isotopes.
Isotopes are atoms of an element that have the same atomic number and varying atomic masses.
4. In any compound, the ratio of the numbers of atoms of any two of the elements present is either an integer or a simple fraction.
Modification: this is not so with some elements like carbon that have the ability to catenate (i.e. form long chains) and build giant molecules like starch.
